Tilman's supplement protocol reflection

Back to supplement table

Published May 24, 2026. Based on Tilman's notes from a professional nutrition consultation and the related transcript.

This is an educational catalog note and personal reflection, not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or a dosing recommendation for other people. Several points below are intentionally marked as open where the consultation notes did not settle them.

My current approach is pragmatic: when there is nothing against taking something in the morning, I prefer the morning because I remember it, drink enough water, and can take fat-soluble supplements with my shake and a teaspoon of olive oil. The consultation notes still create a few clear exceptions: some magnesium forms and calming supplements belong in the evening, collagen should come first on an empty stomach, and a few products are better treated as optional or duplicated.

Magnesium first: three different roles

The consultation separated magnesium by form instead of treating every magnesium product as interchangeable. That is important because the timing and intended use differ.

Magnesium Active Calm

The notes describe Active Calm as a morning-and-evening magnesium product. In the conversation, 120 mg of elemental magnesium was mentioned, and the L-threonate plus acetyltaurate combination was framed as the form most connected to brain, neurotransmitter, and blood-brain-barrier context. For data capture it belongs in the morning filter, but the real routine also includes an evening dose.

Magnesium bisglycinat

Magnesium bisglycinat was clearly placed in the evening. The consultation notes describe it as the more sleep-oriented magnesium form, so I would not treat it like a daytime energy or focus supplement.

Magnesium Complex 11 Ultra XL

The 11-mag complex was placed in the morning and around midday. The notes do not add a deep mechanism here, so I would simply document it as a broader magnesium complex for earlier in the day rather than forcing a stronger claim.

Morning foundation

Most of the routine is easiest to make consistent in the morning. The consultation notes repeatedly say that when morning or midday are both acceptable, the data filter should still be morning because that is the realistic habit.

Collagen Peptides

Collagen is the clearest morning-first item: take it first, on an empty stomach, then wait about 15 to 30 minutes before the other supplements or protein. The notes say absorption was considered much worse when collagen is taken with food. Vitamin C was also mentioned as relevant for collagen effect, which is why vitamin C and zinc came up in the collagen context.

Vitamin B Complex Extra Forte

The B complex was placed in the morning. The consultation notes describe it as a high-dose, water-soluble B-vitamin product connected to cell metabolism, nerves, folate, B12, and homocysteine context. A practical warning was also captured: some people take B vitamins later, but they may affect sleep or dreams, so morning is preferred here.

NADH 50

NADH was placed in the morning and described as similar in direction to Q10: support for the respiratory chain and energy generation, but at a different point in that chain. The notes also connect NAD/NADH with B3/niacin and cell metabolism.

Coenzyme Q10 Kaneka Ubiquinol 200 mg

Q10 was also placed in the morning, one tablet per day. The consultation described the product as high-dose ubiquinol, the active form, and connected it to mental clarity, cellular energy, energy metabolism, and mitochondria. In the notes, mitochondria are described simply as the power plants of the cell.

Hyaluronic Acid 250 mg

Hyaluronic acid was placed in the morning or midday, with the filter set to morning. The notes frame it as a natural moisture-binding factor: a structure that can bind many water molecules and support tissue hydration. Skin, tissue stability, cartilage, and joint-fluid context were mentioned. The product note also matters: this one was described as vegan and fermentation-derived.

Trans-Resveratrol

Resveratrol was placed in the morning or midday with food. The consultation notes describe it as antioxidative and connect polyphenols with immune-system support, but also say the product/source felt somewhat ambiguous. I would keep that caveat visible instead of over-certifying it.

Liposomal Vitamin C + Zinc

The notes did not lock down a perfect daily timing, but the context points toward morning with the rest of the stack. Vitamin C appears especially in the collagen context, and the zinc amount was discussed in relation to total zinc from other products. This is one of the places where the article should stay conservative: useful context, but not a finalized prescription.

Fat-soluble and midday-compatible supplements

Several products can be taken in the morning, especially with the shake and olive oil, but the consultation notes also describe them as morning-or-midday rather than strictly first thing.

Astaxanthin 12 mg BioAstin

Astaxanthin was placed at one per day, morning or midday. The important instruction is to take it with oil or fat. In the conversation, the oil capsule alone was not treated as automatically enough, so taking it with the morning shake and olive oil was considered suitable. The notes describe 12 mg as a high dose and frame astaxanthin as antioxidative in both water and oil phases.

Vitamin D3 + K2 MK-7 tablets

The consultation notes place this at one tablet per week, with two times per week mentioned as a possible context for darker skin types. The conversation connected vitamin D with immune function, UV exposure, liver context, telomere/anti-aging ideas, and hormone-like transcription-factor effects. The cancer/prostate discussion in the transcript is treated here only as background conversation, not as a medical instruction.

Omega-3 / Algae EPA + DHA

The Bryan Johnson omega-3 capsules were noted at two per day. The consultation linked omega-3 with heart and vascular function, brain and attention, eyes and vision, and broader functions. A European oil alternative, NORSAN Omega-3 Vegan/Algae Oil, was discussed as potentially stronger in active content, with oil preferred over capsules in that part of the conversation.

Evening and before-bed supplements

The evening section is smaller but more specific. These are the items where the consultation notes gave a reason not to simply put everything into the morning stack.

L-Theanine Double Strength 200 mg

L-theanine was placed before sleep. The consultation notes only briefly describe it as calming, relaxing, or tiring, so the article should not over-explain it beyond that. Inositol was mentioned as a blood-sugar/insulin-sensitivity topic, but the product's inositol amount was described as too low to expect much from that angle.

Muscle Recover Ashwa Pro Complex

Ashwa Pro was tied to sport and sleep. The notes give two patterns: one capsule after sport and one before bed, or two capsules about 30 minutes before bed if no sport happened. With two capsules, 600 mg ashwagandha was mentioned. The discussion framed ashwagandha around stress, cortisol, and the relationship between cortisol and testosterone, especially after training. Safran was also discussed as mood-related and cortisol/progesterone-related context, but that hormone explanation should be read as consultation context, not a diagnosis.

Blueprint products: keep, replace, or watch for duplication

Longevity Mix

The Longevity Mix was explicitly not something to drop casually. The consultation notes say that it did not seem meaningfully duplicated and that important components are inside: creatine, calcium alpha-ketoglutarate, glucosamine, theanine, glycine, essential amino acids, some magnesium citrate, some hyaluronic acid, taurine, some vitamin C, and allulose. The creatine amount was described as modest compared with common standalone amounts.

Essential Capsules

The Essential Capsules were assessed as something to continue, but consciously. The notes list D3, vitamin E, small amounts of B vitamins, zinc, selenium, manganese, broccoli seed extract, ubiquinol/Q10, spermidine, lithium orotate, and Lactobacillus acidophilus. The B-vitamin doses were compared with the separate B complex and described as very small. Some components may eventually be replaceable, but the exact replacement list was not finalized.

Advanced Antioxidants

Advanced Antioxidants includes vitamin K, lycopene, lutein, and astaxanthin according to the notes. The practical issue is duplication: if separate 12 mg astaxanthin is taken, this product was described as potentially redundant and more like double-spending than a clear additional need.

NAC + Ginger + Curcumin

The notes did not settle a final dose. They describe curcumin and ginger as immune-support context, and NAC as an immune-support and mucus-loosening context. One concrete caution from the transcript: with NAC, drink enough water.

Ashwagandha + Rhodiola

This product was described as less necessary if Ashwa Pro is already in the routine, because the ashwagandha amount was considered much lower than two capsules of Ashwa Pro. Rhodiola was described as an adaptogen for everyday stress adaptation. The notes mention that separate, stronger rhodiola from Sunday Naturals might make more sense later, but this was not finalized.

Open items and deliberate non-recommendations

Fish omega-3

The notes identify fish omega-3 as a gap rather than an active catalog item. NORSAN Omega-3 Total Lemon was mentioned, with at least two times per week and 10 ml as the best mentioned amount. Because this is not yet in the supplement table, it should remain an open action rather than a linked catalog fact.

Probiotics

OMNi-BiOTiC, Sunday Naturals, and Glow25 context came up. For Tilman, OMNi-BiOTiC SR-9 or a nerves-and-psyche context was mentioned as probably relevant. The timing note was clear: empty stomach in water, then stay empty for about 30 minutes. It was also emphasized that probiotics are individual and tied to the gut-brain axis and personal gut context.

Fermented buckwheat

Fermented buckwheat or fermented grains were mentioned as helpful for gut bacteria, with the fermentation process itself framed as relevant. This is a food/gut-health note, not a finalized supplement-kit entry.

Metformin

Metformin came up only as a longevity conversation topic and should not be turned into a recommendation. The notes mention side effects and unclear long-term effects, and explicitly say not to take it over as a recommendation.

Reflection

The biggest learning from this consultation is that the stack should not be viewed as one large pile of pills. It is more useful to separate timing, mechanism, and duplication. Magnesium alone has different roles depending on the form. Antioxidant products can overlap. Morning is best for adherence, but not everything belongs there. Some items are strong candidates to keep; others are watch-list items; a few remain open until the product data or personal need is clearer.

The next step is to keep the supplement table as the exact data layer and use this article as the narrative layer: why the routine is organized this way, what is still uncertain, and what should not be overclaimed.
