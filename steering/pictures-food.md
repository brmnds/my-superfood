# Food Picture Steering

## Goal

Food pictures should make the ingredient immediately recognizable and trustworthy. For the food catalog and landing-page bubbles, show the raw ingredient itself, not a cooked dish, historical document, field scene, package, diagram, or mixed recipe.

## Required Style

- Show the full raw ingredient clearly.
- Use a transparent, white, or very bright neutral background.
- Avoid dark, black, blue, busy, patterned, or kitchen-scene backgrounds.
- Avoid plates unless the plate is plain white and the raw ingredient is still the subject.
- Avoid cooked dishes, soups, prepared meals, or recipe photos for food ingredient cards.
- Avoid cropped ingredients where the user cannot tell what the food is.
- Use consistent product-like lighting: clean, bright, natural, minimal shadows.
- Prefer isolated product photography or generated product-style imagery when a clean licensed photo is unavailable.

## Ingredient-Specific Standards

- Broccoli: raw broccoli florets or head, fully visible, clean bright background.
- Chickpeas: full real chickpeas, dry or cooked whole chickpeas are acceptable; do not use chickpea flour, hummus, or powder.
- Lentils: clean uncooked lentils, preferably small piles or a simple bowl/cup; do not use lentil soup or cooked dishes.
- Cauliflower: raw cauliflower head/florets, no field, people, labels, or document background; transparent or bright background.
- Banana: fully visible bananas with no visible rectangular background; transparent background preferred.
- Avocado: full/halved avocado is acceptable if cleanly presented.
- Blueberries: whole blueberries, clean close-up, no unrelated dish.
- Spinach: raw spinach leaves, clean bright background.
- Salmon: a clean raw salmon fillet/piece; do not use a salmon dish, plated meal, or cooked recipe.
- Oats: oats as a small mountain, scoop, white bowl, or small cup on a bright background; no blue or busy background.
- Olive oil: clear bottle or small dish of oil on bright neutral background.
- Walnuts: raw walnut halves/pieces on bright neutral background.
- Yogurt: protein yogurt in a clean white bowl/cup; avoid unclear vegan yogurt, packaging, or messy plated scenes.
- Sweet potato: raw sweet potato, whole or cut, clean bright background; do not use cooked dishes.
- Cacao: cacao powder/nibs in a clean bowl, spoon, or small pile with bright neutral background.
- Pumpkin seeds: pumpkin seeds on transparent, white, or bright neutral background; no black background.

## Validation Rule

Before accepting a food image, check:

1. Is it the raw ingredient?
2. Is the ingredient fully visible and recognizable?
3. Is the background transparent, white, or bright neutral?
4. Is there no cooked dish, document, packaging, field scene, or dark background?
5. Does it look good in both the landing-page circle and the catalog card crop?

If any answer is no, mark the asset as failing and replace it before deployment.
